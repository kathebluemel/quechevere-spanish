Unit 5: Food & Preferences

Talk about foods you like and don’t like, make simple requests, and order politely using high-frequency phrases.

Proficiency: Novice Mid → Novice High (supported) High-frequency food words Me gusta / No me gusta + polite ordering

Proficiency Goal

Students understand and communicate basic food preferences and simple ordering in familiar contexts using practiced language.

Essential Question

¿Cómo influyen la cultura y la familia en lo que comemos?

Core Language Targets

  • I can say what I like and don’t like (me gusta / no me gusta).
  • I can ask and answer simple preference questions.
  • I can order politely using short phrases.
  • I can understand a simple menu and choose items.
Unit 5 “Must-Have” Language
Likes/Dislikes: Me gusta(n)…, No me gusta(n)…, Me encanta(n)…, Prefiero…
Food: pizza, tacos, ensalada, arroz, pollo, pescado, pan, fruta, verduras, helado, agua, jugo, leche
Ordering: Quiero…, Para mí…, ¿Cuánto cuesta?, La cuenta, por favor, ¿Me trae…?, Gracias

Stage 1: Understand
Interpretive • Comprehension first
▾
  • I can understand what foods someone likes and dislikes.
  • I can identify key menu words and prices.
  • I can match preference statements to meaning.
Lectura (10+ sentences)

Hoy es viernes y yo tengo hambre. En la cafetería hay pizza, ensalada, arroz y pollo. A mí me gusta la pizza, pero no me gusta la ensalada. Mi amiga Carla prefiere el arroz y el pollo. Ella dice: “Me encanta el pollo.” Yo quiero pizza y agua. Carla quiere arroz y jugo. En la mesa, hablamos de la comida en casa. En mi casa comemos fruta después de la cena. Carla come helado los sábados. Al final, decimos: “¡Qué rico!”

Comprehension (answer in English)
  1. Where are they eating?
  2. What foods are available?
  3. What does the narrator like and not like?
  4. What does Carla prefer?
  5. What does each person drink?
  6. What do they eat at home after dinner / on Saturdays?
Stage 2: Interact
Interpersonal • Ask & answer with support
▾
Core Q/A Frames
  • ¿Te gusta…? → Sí, me gusta… / No, no me gusta…
  • ¿Qué te gusta comer? → Me gusta comer…
  • ¿Qué prefieres? → Prefiero…
  • ¿Qué quieres? → Quiero… / Para mí…
Interpersonal Tasks
  • Food survey: ask 5 classmates “¿Te gusta…?” then tally results.
  • Restaurant role-play: partner A orders, partner B is the server.
  • This or that: “¿Prefieres pizza o tacos?” justify with one simple reason (porque es…).
Stage 3: Create
Presentational • Make a food product
▾
Presentational Options
  • My Menu: create a mini menu with 6 items + prices + 5 preference sentences.
  • Food profile: 8–10 sentences about what you like, don’t like, and what you eat at home.
  • Ordering audio: 30–45 second recording ordering a meal politely.
Stage 4: Reflect
Culture + Metacognition
▾
  • What foods feel “everyday” in different cultures?
  • How do people show politeness when ordering?
  • What is one phrase I can use when I forget a word (gesture + “¿Cómo se dice…?”)?
Stage 5: Perform
Assessment • Balanced across modes
▾
  • Interpretive: short menu + preference text + English questions
  • Interpersonal: ordering role-play (server/customer)
  • Presentational: food profile OR menu project

Standards

Standards: ACTFL – Interpretive, Interpersonal, Presentational | MO WL.1 & WL.3
