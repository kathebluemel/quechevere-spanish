Numbers & Classroom Language

Focus: numbers (0–31) + classroom survival phrases to ask for help, clarify meaning, and follow routines. Goal: stay in Spanish and keep communication going.

Unit Goal + Performance Task

Proficiency target: Novice Low → Novice Mid

Student-friendly goal: By the end of this unit, I can use numbers and classroom phrases to ask for help and understand directions.

Essential Question: How do we use polite routines to learn and communicate in class?

End-of-Unit Performance Task (Preview):

Complete a short “classroom survival” role-play: ask for help, ask what a word means, request repetition, and answer 3 number questions (0–31). Goal: stay in Spanish using strategies when you get stuck.

Success = communication continues. Accuracy is emerging.

Stage 1 — Understand (Interpretive)

Your job: understand classroom phrases and identify numbers (0–31) in context.

Mini Text (semi-authentic classroom moment)

Hoy es martes. En la clase de español, la profesora dice: “Abran el cuaderno.” Un estudiante pregunta: “¿Qué significa ‘tarea’?” La profesora responde y el estudiante dice: “Gracias.” Después, la profesora pregunta: “¿Cuántos estudiantes hay?” Hay veintidós. Otro estudiante dice: “No entiendo. ¿Puede repetir, por favor?”

Stage 2 — Interact (Interpersonal)

Your job: ask for help and negotiate meaning using classroom rejoinders.

Core exchange (practice aloud)

A: No entiendo. ¿Puede repetir, por favor?
B: Sí. (Repito.)
A: Gracias. ¿Qué significa “_____”?
B: Significa “_____”.
A: Ah, ok. ¿Cómo se dice “_____” en español?

Goal: keep interaction going.

Rejoinders

  • No entiendo.
  • Más lento, por favor.
  • Repita, por favor.
  • ¿Qué significa…?
  • ¿Cómo se dice…?

Stage 3 — Create (Presentational)

Your job: create a “Classroom Survival Card” you can use in class.

Classroom Survival Card (Spanish)

  • Hola / Buenos días.
  • No entiendo.
  • Más lento, por favor.
  • Repita, por favor.
  • ¿Qué significa _____?
  • ¿Cómo se dice _____ en español?
  • Gracias.

Goal: useful, clear, and ready to use.

Stage 4 — Reflect (Culture + Growth)

Your job: connect classroom routines to culture and reflect on learning strategies.

Cultural Connection (Products • Practices • Perspectives)

  • Product: Polite phrases (por favor, gracias) are common in school interactions.
  • Practice: Students often use respectful routines when asking for help.
  • Perspective: Respect and community can be emphasized in classroom culture.

Stage 5 — Perform (Assessment)

Complete the performance task. Focus on staying in Spanish and completing the requirements.

Performance Task: Classroom Survival Role-Play

  • Ask for repetition (Repita…, Más lento…)
  • Ask meaning (¿Qué significa…?)
  • Ask how to say a word (¿Cómo se dice…?)
  • Answer 3 number questions (0–31)
  • Use polite language (por favor, gracias)

Teacher Alignment & Standards (click to expand)

ACTFL Modes Alignment

  • Interpretive: Students interpret classroom moments for gist, strategies, and numbers in context.
  • Interpersonal: Students negotiate meaning using rejoinders and polite routines.
  • Presentational: Students create a functional “Survival Card” and demonstrate number use.

Proficiency Target

  • Novice Low → Novice Mid (formulaic classroom language + emerging control; communication continues).

Can-Do Statements (Teacher-facing)

  • I can interpret short classroom interactions and identify key information, including numbers.
  • I can ask for repetition, ask meaning, and request clarification to stay in the target language.
  • I can use numbers 0–31 in simple classroom contexts (age/date/quantity).
  • I can reflect on classroom routines and cultural perspectives around politeness/respect.

Suggested Pacing

  • 5–8 instructional days (flexible): Stage 1 (2), Stage 2 (2), Stage 3 (1–2), Stage 4 (1), Stage 5 (1).
