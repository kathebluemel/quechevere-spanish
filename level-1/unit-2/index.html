Unit 2: Numbers & Classroom Language

Use numbers (0–31) and classroom survival phrases to ask for help, clarify meaning, and keep communication going.

Proficiency: Novice Low → Novice Mid Classroom routines Polite communication strategies

Proficiency Goal

Students understand and use basic numbers and classroom phrases to participate, respond, and stay in Spanish with support.

Essential Question

¿Cómo usamos rutinas para aprender y comunicar en clase?

Core Language Targets

  • I can understand and say numbers 0–31.
  • I can ask for help and clarification politely.
  • I can follow common classroom directions.
  • I can use strategies to stay in Spanish.
Unit 2 “Must-Have” Language
Numbers: 0–31 (cero, uno, dos… treinta y uno)
Survival phrases: ¿Cómo se dice…?, No entiendo, ¿Puede repetir?, Más despacio, Necesito ayuda, ¿Qué significa…?, Perdón
Directions: Escuchen, Miren, Repitan, Lean, Escriban, Saquen…, Guarden…

Stage 1: Understand
Interpretive • Comprehension first
▾
  • I can recognize numbers in context (dates, quantities).
  • I can understand common classroom commands.
  • I can match survival phrases to meaning.
Lectura (10+ sentences)

En la clase de español, la profesora dice: “Buenos días.” Los estudiantes responden: “Buenos días.” Hoy es el día quince y hay veintidós estudiantes. La profesora dice: “Saquen un lápiz y una hoja.” Después dice: “Escuchen y repitan.” Un estudiante pregunta: “¿Puede repetir, por favor?” Otro estudiante dice: “No entiendo.” La profesora habla más despacio y ayuda. Luego los estudiantes escriben los números del cero al treinta y uno. Al final, guardan los materiales y dicen: “Hasta luego.”

Comprehension (answer in English)
  1. What day of the month is it?
  2. How many students are there?
  3. What materials do students take out?
  4. What does the teacher ask students to do?
  5. Which two survival phrases do students use?
  6. What do students do at the end of class?
Stage 2: Interact
Interpersonal • Ask & answer with support
▾
Core Q/A Frames
  • ¿Cómo se dice… en español? → Se dice…
  • ¿Qué significa…? → Significa…
  • ¿Puede repetir? → Sí, repito…
  • ¿Cuántos… hay? → Hay…
Interpersonal Tasks
  • Number partner quiz: say a number, partner writes it.
  • Classroom role-play: student asks for help/clarification using 2 phrases.
  • Info gap: partner A has “date + quantity,” partner B asks questions to fill in.
Stage 3: Create
Presentational • Classroom survival demo
▾
Presentational Options
  • Mini skit: 30–45 seconds demonstrating 3 classroom phrases + 3 numbers.
  • Poster: “Stay in Spanish” survival phrase chart.
  • Audio: count 0–31 + 3 phrases.
Stage 4: Reflect
Culture + Metacognition
▾
  • Which phrase helps me the most when I’m stuck?
  • How do routines help a class communicate more?
  • What will I try next time: repeat, slower, or “what does it mean”?
Stage 5: Perform
Assessment • Balanced across modes
▾
  • Interpretive: classroom scenario + English questions
  • Interpersonal: role-play using survival phrases
  • Presentational: numbers + survival phrases demonstration
