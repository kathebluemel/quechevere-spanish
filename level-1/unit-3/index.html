Unit 3: Descriptions & Adjectives

Describe people and objects using ser + adjectives, basic agreement (o/a/s), and high-frequency descriptors.

Proficiency: Novice Low → Novice Mid High-frequency adjectives Agreement + clarity first

Proficiency Goal

Students interpret and create clear descriptions using simple adjective phrases. Meaning first; accuracy grows over time.

Essential Question

¿Cómo reflejan las descripciones lo que una cultura nota y valora?

Core Language Targets

  • I can describe a person with 3+ adjectives.
  • I can describe an object with color/size/quality.
  • I can use adjective agreement in basic ways.
  • I can understand descriptions in short texts.
Unit 3 “Must-Have” Language
Ser: soy, eres, es, somos, son
Common adjectives: alto/a, bajo/a, grande, pequeño/a, bonito/a, feo/a, simpático/a, amable, inteligente, atlético/a, tímido/a, divertido/a
Colors: rojo/a, azul, verde, amarillo/a, negro/a, blanco/a

Stage 1: Understand
Interpretive • Comprehension first
▾
  • I can identify adjectives and what they describe.
  • I can understand basic agreement cues (o/a/s).
  • I can match descriptions to people/objects.
Lectura (10+ sentences)

En la escuela hay un estudiante nuevo. Él se llama Diego y es muy simpático. Diego es alto y atlético. Tiene una mochila grande y negra. En su clase hay una chica que se llama Ana. Ana es inteligente y amable. Ella tiene una carpeta azul y un cuaderno pequeño. Los dos estudiantes son muy trabajadores. Después de clase, Diego es feliz porque tiene nuevos amigos. Ana también está contenta.

Comprehension (answer in English)
  1. Who is the new student and how is he described?
  2. List two adjectives for Diego.
  3. What color is Diego’s backpack?
  4. Who is Ana and how is she described?
  5. What items does Ana have?
  6. How do both students feel after class?
Stage 2: Interact
Interpersonal • Ask & answer with support
▾
Core Q/A Frames
  • ¿Cómo es? → Es…
  • ¿De qué color es? → Es…
  • ¿Es grande o pequeño/a? → Es…
  • ¿Quién es? → Es…
Interpersonal Tasks
  • Guess Who? Describe a person; partner guesses.
  • Lost & Found info gap: describe an item to locate its owner.
  • Partner compare: describe two objects and say which you prefer.
Stage 3: Create
Presentational • Make a description product
▾
Presentational Options
  • Lost & Found description: write/record a missing item description + a person description.
  • Character card: 8–10 sentences describing a person.
  • Object gallery: 6 labeled objects with adjectives and colors.
Stage 4: Reflect
Culture + Metacognition
▾
  • What kinds of descriptions feel “important” in different cultures?
  • What strategy helps me use agreement (o/a/s) without overthinking?
  • How can I make my meaning clearer with simple words?
Stage 5: Perform
Assessment • Balanced across modes
▾
  • Interpretive: new description text + English questions
  • Interpersonal: “Guess Who / Lost & Found” partner task
  • Presentational: description product with checklist

Standards
