Unit 7: Family & Relationships

Talk about family members, describe people in your world, and ask/answer simple questions about relationships using high-frequency language.
Proficiency: Novice Mid → Novice High (supported) High-frequency descriptions Mi / tu + family words

Proficiency Goal

Students understand and communicate about family members and relationships in familiar contexts using short, practiced phrases and simple sentences (Novice-Mid performance target).

Essential Question

¿Quiénes son las personas importantes en mi vida y por qué?

Core Language Targets

  • I can name family members (madre, padre, hermano/a, abuelo/a, etc.).
  • I can say who someone is (Es mi… / Es su…).
  • I can describe a person using basic adjectives (alto/a, simpático/a, trabajador/a, etc.).
  • I can ask and answer simple relationship questions (¿Quién es…? / ¿Cómo es…?).
Unit 7 “Must-Have” Language
Family: madre, padre, mamá, papá, hermano/a, hijo/a, abuelo/a, tío/a, primo/a, familia, amigo/a
Relationships: mi, tu, su, de, con, y; ¿Quién es…?, Es mi… / Es su…, Tengo…, Vive en…
Descriptions: alto/a, bajo/a, joven, mayor, simpático/a, serio/a, trabajador/a, divertido/a, inteligente

Stage 1: Understand
Interpretive • Comprehension first
▼
  • I can understand who people are in a short text.
  • I can identify family words and basic descriptions.
  • I can match “Es mi…” statements to meaning.
Lectura (10+ sentences)
En mi casa hay cinco personas. Mi madre se llama Ana y es muy simpática. Mi padre se llama Carlos y es trabajador. Tengo un hermano y una hermana. Mi hermano se llama Luis y es alto. Mi hermana se llama Sofía y es inteligente. Mis abuelos viven cerca y son muy amables. A veces, mi tío viene los sábados y cocina para la familia. Mi primo juega deportes y es divertido. En mi familia, todos hablamos y reímos juntos.
Comprehension (answer in English)
  1. How many people are in the house?
  2. What is the mother’s name?
  3. Which person is described as “trabajador”?
  4. Who is “alto”?
  5. Where do the grandparents live?
  6. What does the uncle do on Saturdays?
Stage 2: Interact
Interpersonal • Ask & answer with support
▼
Partner Mini-Conversation (use the frames)
A: ¿Quién es?
B: Es mi ________.

A: ¿Cómo es?
B: Es ________ (simpático/a, trabajador/a, divertido/a…).

A: ¿Vive contigo?
B: Sí / No. Vive en ________.

A: ¡Qué bien! / ¡Interesante!
Quick Check
Ask and answer about 3 people: (1) your family, (2) a friend, (3) a classmate’s family (with permission).
Stage 3: Create
Presentational • Short writing/speaking
▼
My People (6–8 sentences)
Write about 3 important people in your life. Include:
  • Who they are (Es mi… / Es mi amigo/a…)
  • 2 descriptions for each person
  • One place they live or one thing they like
Stage 4: Reflect
Culture + Metacognition
▼
Reflection (English is OK here)
How do families show care in different cultures (food, time together, greetings, celebrations)? Write 3–5 sentences. Include one connection to Spanish-speaking cultures you have learned about.
Stage 5: Perform
Assessment • Balanced across modes
▼
Performance Task (Preview)
Record a 30–45 second video: “These are my people.” Include 3 people, 2 descriptions each, and 1 simple comparison sentence starter:

En mi familia, ________. En (país/cultura), ________.

Goal: be understood using clear, practiced phrases (Novice-Mid performance).
