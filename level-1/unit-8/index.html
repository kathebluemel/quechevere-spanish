Unit 8: La Vida Escolar

Talk about your classes, your schedule, what you need for school, and how your school day shapes your identity — then compare it to students in the Spanish-speaking world.

Proficiency Target: Novice-Mid IPA: Interpretive + Interpersonal + Presentational School Products & Practices (Comparisons) Porque + Sequencing Words

Standards

MO / National WL: Communication, Cultures, Comparisons
Interpersonal (IL.1.1): exchange info about schedule & preferences • Interpretive (IL.1.2): understand an authentic schedule • Presentational (IL.1.3): basic comparison of school day • Cultural Comparisons (IL.4.2): products/practices (uniforms, recreo, grading)

Proficiency Goal

Students communicate about classes, time, and school routines using practiced phrases and simple sentences, and they make a basic cultural comparison using familiar vocabulary.

Essential Question

¿Cómo influye mi escuela en quién soy y cómo me comparo con otros estudiantes del mundo?

Learning Targets (Can-Do)

  • I can list my classes and the times they meet using numbers 0–100.
  • I can describe my teachers and classes using at least 3 distinct adjectives.
  • I can express what I need for specific classes and what I do in them.
  • I can justify likes/dislikes using porque.
  • I can identify 2–3 major differences between U.S. schools and schools in the Spanish-speaking world.

Functional Toolbox (Vocab & Grammar)

Subjects: matemáticas, ciencias (naturales/sociales), español, inglés, arte, tecnología, educación física, almuerzo/recreo
Gear: mochila, mapa, calculadora, sitio web, pantalla, ratón, tijeras, regla
People: estudiante, profesor(a), director(a)

Engine: tener (Tengo la clase de…), estar (El libro está en…), -AR verbs (estudiar, hablar, escuchar, dibujar, usar, sacar una buena nota)
Sequencing: primero, luego, después, al final • Connectors: y, pero, también, porque
Teacher Alignment (click to show/hide)

Rigor moves (Novice-Mid): prioritize message success; do not over-penalize “Yo tener” vs “Yo tengo” if communication works.

  • 90% TL: use visuals/icons for gear (mochila, lápiz, calculadora) to reduce English.
  • Avoid word searches: use “shopping list” tasks (What do you buy for Arte vs Matemáticas?).
  • Comparison done safely: keep culture talk anchored to concrete products/practices (uniforms, recreo, grading).

Interpretive Reading (10+ sentences)

Horario escolar (Puebla, México):

Me llamo Sofía y soy estudiante en Puebla. Primero, tengo matemáticas a las 7:10. Luego tengo ciencias a las 8:00. Después, tengo español a las 8:50 y me gusta porque la profesora es simpática. A las 9:40 tengo recreo y estoy con mis amigos. Después del recreo, tengo historia a las 10:00 y usamos un mapa en clase. Luego tengo inglés a las 10:50. A las 11:40 tengo tecnología y uso la computadora y la pantalla. Al final, tengo educación física a las 12:30 y necesito zapatos deportivos. En mi escuela, muchos estudiantes usan uniforme y la calificación es de 1 a 10.

Comprehension (answer in English)

  1. What time does Sofía have a break (recreo)?
  2. Which class does she have first?
  3. Why does she like Spanish class?
  4. What two pieces of technology does she use in tecnología?
  5. What does she need for physical education?
  6. Name one “product” and one “practice” mentioned about school culture.
  7. How is the grading scale described?
Stage 2: Interact
Interpersonal • Ask & answer with support

Schedule Swap (Interpersonal)

Scenario: Student A is new. Student B is the “Ambassador.” Talk for 3 minutes.

Use these question frames:
  • ¿Qué clase tienes primero?
  • ¿A qué hora tienes (matemáticas / ciencias / español)?
  • ¿Te gusta…? ¿Por qué?
  • ¿Qué necesitas para (arte / matemáticas / tecnología)?
  • ¿Qué haces en esa clase?

Sentence Builders (guided → more spontaneous)

Build 5 sentences:
Tengo ___ a las ___. Me gusta ___ porque ___.
En ___, yo ___ (estudio / hablo / escucho / dibujo / uso…).
Necesito ___ y ___. Primero ___. Luego ___. Al final ___.

Presentational Writing

Write 6–8 sentences about your school day. Include:
  • 3 classes + times (numbers 0–100)
  • 3 adjectives (teacher/class)
  • At least 1 sentence with porque
  • At least 2 school items you need (mochila, calculadora, etc.)
  • At least 2 sequencing words (primero, luego, después, al final)

Cultural Comparisons (IL.4.2)

Create a quick comparison (Venn diagram or bullets):
  • Products: uniforms, supplies, school ID, notebooks, etc.
  • Practices: recreo, lunch schedule, grading scale (1–10), routines
Goal: Identify 2–3 major differences using simple Spanish phrases you know.
Helpful frames:
En mi escuela, ___. En Puebla, ___.
En los EE. UU., ___. Pero en México, ___.
Prefiero ___ porque ___.
Stage 5: Perform
Assessment • IPA (Gold Standard)
▾

Integrated Performance Assessment (IPA)

Theme: The Exchange Student Arrival
Part 1 — Interpretive (Reading):
Read an authentic schedule from a Spanish-speaking country (like Puebla). Answer questions in English:
  • At what time does the student have a break?
  • Which class meets every day?
  • How does the grading scale differ from yours?
Part 2 — Interpersonal (Speaking):
“Schedule Swap” (3 minutes). Ask about favorite classes and what you do in them. Find out what you both need to buy at the store today.
Part 3 — Presentational (Video or Digital Brochure):
“Vlog de Bienvenida” (about 1 minute or a simple digital brochure):
  • Describe a typical Monday (mention 3 classes + times)
  • Describe 3 teachers/classes using adjectives
  • Say what you carry in your backpack
  • Say 1 thing unique about your school compared to theirs
